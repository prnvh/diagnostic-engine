How you can use it

Start from the complaint you actually have, not from a rigid checklist.

The flow below is organized the way people describe knee problems in the real world: after a twist, after weeks of stairs pain, when the joint catches, or when something feels unsafe.

Sports and awkward movement

Best for acute injury stories where timing and mechanism matter.

These starts help the engine ask about pivoting, swelling timing, instability, and whether you had to stop right away.

Gradual or repetitive strain

Useful when the story is less about one event and more about a pattern over time.

These prompts emphasize stairs, sitting, running, morning stiffness, grinding, and longer timelines.

Motion problems

Helpful when the complaint is about movement quality, not just pain.

Catching, locking, buckling, reduced trust in the knee, and loss of motion often drive the next question batch.

Do not bury safety signals

Use this to test where the engine stops ranking and tells you to get urgent care.

The current safety layer is global. It looks for hot and red knee with fever, deformity, and major trauma with severe weight-bearing difficulty.

How it works

A bounded loop, not an endless questionnaire.

The engine does not ask everything. It parses the complaint, scores structured disease nodes, and selects only the next questions that are worth asking.

01

Free text in

The parser reads the complaint and maps only what it can justify into the knee symptom registry. Unclear leftovers stay unparsed rather than being hallucinated into the model.

02

Targeted questions out

The selector looks at the live shortlist, unresolved evidence, and question conditions, then picks a small batch instead of exhausting the whole questionnaire.

03

Bounded result

The session ends in one of three ways: a confident shortlist, a cautious fallback, or a safety escalation when the red-flag layer interrupts the normal ranking loop.

04

Audit trail preserved

Major session events are written to an append-only ledger so the reasoning path can be inspected after the interview instead of disappearing inside an opaque response.

Why this works

More governed than a generic symptom chatbot.

The product is intentionally narrow and inspectable. It is designed to demonstrate structured reasoning, not broad open-domain medical advice.

Registry-bound

Medical logic lives in explicit disease nodes.

Symptoms, questions, and disease patterns are all versioned in JSON registries rather than hidden in prompt text or client-side heuristics.

Evidence-aware

Unknown stays unknown.

The scorer does not treat missing evidence as false. It weights explicit answers more strongly than inferred clues and can hard-block nodes only when the contradiction is actually confirmed.

Safety-first

Red flags sit outside disease ranking.

Deformity, hot or red knee with fever, and major trauma with severe weight-bearing difficulty trigger escalation because those patterns should not stay inside a normal shortlist loop.

Deployable MVP

Serverless frontend, durable backend.

The site runs on Vercel, persists sessions in Supabase, and keeps the reasoning core deterministic whether it is hosted locally or as a public demo.
