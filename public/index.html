What this site actually does

Not a broad chatbot. A bounded reasoning loop.

It takes free text seriously.

The first complaint shapes the interview instead of forcing a full checklist.

It stays inside a controlled registry.

Unknown facts remain unknown rather than being quietly invented into the model.

It can stop early for safety.

Hot red knee with fever, deformity, or major trauma can interrupt normal ranking.

How it works

The system is easier to understand when you look at the loop directly.

01

Parse the complaint

The parser reads the initial story and maps only what it can justify into the knee symptom registry. Anything unclear stays unresolved rather than being guessed.

  • Free text in
  • Controlled symptom evidence out
  • Unparsed leftovers preserved
02

Ask only the next useful questions

The engine scores the live candidate pool, looks for missing high-value evidence, and chooses a small batch of follow-up questions instead of asking everything.

  • Question conditions are respected
  • Low-confidence clues can be clarified
  • Each round is intentionally narrow
03

Stop with a bounded result

The loop ends when the shortlist is strong enough, when evidence stays too thin and the system falls back, or when safety logic says the normal ranking loop should stop.

  • Shortlist when fit is strong
  • Fallback when confidence is not earned
  • Escalation when red flags appear

Outputs

The engine has only three endings, and they mean different things.

Shortlist

When the symptom pattern is strong enough, the engine returns ranked fit scores with a best stage and the strongest supporting and penalizing features.

Fallback

When the structured evidence is still too weak or too ambiguous, the system does not bluff. It stops with a bounded fallback and shows the closest fits so far.

Safety escalation

When the pattern crosses urgent safety rules, the disease-ranking loop is interrupted and the result becomes a direct escalation message.
